# 4. Scope of SEO Services

## 4.1 Initial SEO Discovery and Baseline Review

Provider may conduct an initial review of Client’s online presence, including:

- Website structure, navigation, indexing status, crawl accessibility, and technical obstacles.
- Available Google Search Console and Google Analytics data, when Client grants access.
- Page titles, meta descriptions, headings, content structure, internal linking, and basic conversion paths.
- Mobile usability, performance concerns, technical errors, and user experience observations.
- Local search presence, competitor observations, and backlink risk indicators, when included in the plan.

**Deliverable:** Initial findings summary and recommended priority action plan.

## 4.2 Keyword and Search Intent Research

Provider may identify relevant search topics and keyword opportunities based on Client’s actual services, products, audience, geography, and competitive environment. This may include primary service keywords, location-based queries, informational topics, page-to-keyword mapping, and content opportunities.

Provider will not intentionally target misleading, irrelevant, or deceptive terms.

## 4.3 On-Page SEO Optimization

Provider may optimize existing pages or prepare recommendations for approval, including:

- SEO title tags and meta descriptions.
- Heading structure, readability, page relevance, and call-to-action placement.
- Image alt text recommendations and internal linking improvements.
- Service page, product page, location page, and FAQ optimization where appropriate.
- Schema markup recommendations or implementation where technically supported and appropriate.

Provider will not publish unsupported claims, fabricated credentials, fake locations, or deceptive business information.

## 4.4 Technical SEO

Depending on the selected plan and access granted, technical SEO work may include review or implementation of:

- XML sitemaps, robots.txt, indexing, crawlability, canonical URLs, and redirects.
- Search Console issues, broken links, duplicate content observations, and internal site architecture.
- Mobile usability, basic performance observations, Core Web Vitals observations, and structured data.

Work requiring custom programming, hosting or server changes, premium tools, theme changes, major redesign, or third-party development is excluded unless expressly included or separately approved in writing.

## 4.5 Content Strategy and Content Creation

When included in the selected plan, Provider may prepare or optimize written website content such as service pages, product category pages, genuine local landing pages, blog articles, FAQs, homepage copy, about-page copy, conversion copy, and image SEO metadata.

When selected, Provider may assist with Google Business Profile review, business name/address/phone consistency, category and service-area recommendations, local page strategy, review request process recommendations, and citation consistency observations.
